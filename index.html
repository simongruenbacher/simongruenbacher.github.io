My name is Simon Grünbacher. I am currently in the master's programme for computer science at Johannes Kepler University Linz, Austria. During my bachelor's degree I was employed at the Institute for Systems Software at JKU, working on the development of JavaWiz. It's a visual Java Debugger primarily intended for first-semester computer science students. The Frontend is written in VueJS and TypeScript, the Backend in Kotlin.

During my bachelor's, I was also working at the Institute for Algebra and the Institute for Integrated Circuits at JKU. At the Institute for Algebra, I was employed in the FWF Project Equations in Universal Algebra under the supervision of Erhard Aichinger. The aim of the project was to investigate the computational complexity of solving equations over finite structures such as groups and rings. At the Institute for Integrated Circuits, I was working on a combinatorial optimization problem under the supervision of Richard Kueng.
